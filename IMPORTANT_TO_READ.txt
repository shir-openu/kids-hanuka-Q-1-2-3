QUESTION 1 - CORRECT ANSWERS
================================================================================

Given: ∠A₁ = ∠A₂ = ∠A₃ = 20°, and at E marked 75°

a. ∠ADE = 55°
   Explanation: Adjacent angles on a line sum to 180°

b. ∠ACD = 35°
   Explanation: Sum of angles in triangle ACD = 180°

c. ∠ABC = 15°
   Explanation: Sum of angles in triangle ABC = 180°, ∠BAC = 60°

================================================================================
QUESTION 2 - CORRECT ANSWERS
================================================================================

Given: ∠A = 50°, right angle at D, ∠BDC = 70°

a. ∠CAD = 20°
   Explanation: AD bisects angle A, so ∠CAD = part of ∠BAC

b. ∠ABE = 20°
   Explanation: In triangle ABD, ∠ADB = 90°, sum = 180°

c. ∠BFD = 70°
   Explanation: Sum of angles in triangle BFD = 180°

d. ∠DFE = 110°
   Explanation: ∠BFD and ∠DFE are adjacent angles on a line (180° - 70° = 110°)

================================================================================
QUESTION 3 - CORRECT ANSWERS
================================================================================

Given: Isosceles triangle AB = AC, base angles = 64°, AK bisects ∠DAC

a. ∠BAC = 52°
   Explanation: Sum of angles in triangle = 180°, so 180° - 64° - 64° = 52°

b. ∠DAC = 128°
   Explanation: External angle = 180° - internal angle = 180° - 52° = 128°

c. ∠KAC = 64°
   Explanation: AK bisects ∠DAC, so 128° ÷ 2 = 64°

d. PROOF: BC || AK
   Because ∠KAC = ∠ACB = 64° (alternate angles are equal → parallel lines)
